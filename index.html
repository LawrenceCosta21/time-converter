What is Unix time?

Unix time (epoch time) counts the number of seconds that have elapsed since January 1, 1970 00:00:00 UTC — also known as the Unix epoch. It's the universal language of timestamps in software.

Seconds vs milliseconds

Most Unix systems use seconds (10-digit numbers like 1750000000). JavaScript and many modern APIs use milliseconds (13-digit numbers like 1750000000000).

Year 2038 problem

32-bit systems storing Unix time as a signed integer will overflow on January 19, 2038. Modern 64-bit systems can represent dates hundreds of billions of years into the future.
